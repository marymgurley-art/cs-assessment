SEL Assessment Landscape

Competitor teardown — what is each tool actually building, who are they building it for, and where do they fall short for classroom teachers?

📌 Scope note:  Intellispark Pro (CharacterStrong's active integration, launched Winter 2025) is excluded. Focus here is the broader market.
Competitor Teardowns
Panorama Education
panoramaed.com · District SEL analytics
Provides districts with SEL survey tools and data dashboards that help administrators measure student social-emotional well-being at scale. Used by thousands of districts to report SEL outcomes to school boards and funders. The primary audience is district leadership, not classroom teachers.
🏢 District tool
📐 Grade range
K–12
👤 Primary user
Administrators and district leaders — teachers collect data but aren't the audience
📝 How assessment works
Student-completed SEL surveys (10–20 questions), administered 2–3× per year. Measures competencies like self-management and growth mindset.
📊 Reporting & data
District-wide dashboards for trends by school, grade, and demographic group. Built for leadership presentations, not daily instruction.
📚 Tied to curriculum?
No  Surveys are standalone — completely disconnected from what teachers are actually teaching
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
Limited  Aggregate data can inform school-level tier decisions but doesn't identify individual students for support
✨ Unique features
Demographic disaggregation (race, gender, ELL) · Family and staff surveys alongside student surveys · Longitudinal trend reporting across school years
Key Gap
Solves the district reporting problem, not the teacher instruction problem. 2–3× per year cadence misses every instructional moment. Teachers get no guidance on what to do after seeing results. Fundamentally an admin tool.
Aperture Education (DESSA)
apertureed.com · MTSS SEL screening
The Devereux Student Strengths Assessment (DESSA) is a standardized, norm-referenced SEL screening tool used in MTSS frameworks. Gives schools research-backed data for identifying students who need Tier 2 or Tier 3 SEL intervention — primarily used by counselors, psychologists, and MTSS teams, not classroom teachers.
🏥 Specialist tool
📐 Grade range
K–8 (DESSA); Pre-K and high school versions also available
👤 Primary user
School counselors, psychologists, MTSS specialists — teachers complete ratings, but results flow to intervention teams
📝 How assessment works
Teachers complete a 72-item behavioral rating scale per student, 3× per year. Scores against national norms to identify students above or below benchmark.
📊 Reporting & data
Individual student SEL profiles across 8 competency areas. Designed for IEP documentation and intervention planning — not real-time instructional decisions.
📚 Tied to curriculum?
No  Purely a screening tool — identifies gaps but provides no guidance on how to address them through instruction
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
Yes — core purpose  Best-in-class for formal MTSS identification and documentation. Weak on what to do after identifying a need.
✨ Unique features
Norm-referenced national benchmarks defensible for IEP/legal use · Strengths-based framing (measures assets, not just deficits) · Research-validated across 15+ years
Key Gap
Designed for identification, not instruction. Rating every student quarterly is a significant teacher burden — and after completing it, teachers get no actionable guidance for their classroom. Tells you who needs help; doesn't help you help them.
Second Step
secondstep.org · SEL curriculum (direct competitor)
K–8 evidence-based SEL curriculum from Committee for Children — the closest structural competitor to CharacterStrong. Provides full lesson plans, student materials, and teacher guides organized around four core SEL competency areas. Digital versions include basic embedded comprehension checks, making this the most instructive comparison for what CharacterStrong could build — and do better.
📗 SEL curriculum
📐 Grade range
Pre-K – Grade 8; high school version sold separately
👤 Primary user
Classroom teachers — the most direct analog to CharacterStrong's user
📝 How assessment works
Low-fidelity embedded checks within digital lesson modules — mostly multiple choice comprehension questions. Not a systematic assessment framework; varies by grade and unit. No class-wide data view.
📊 Reporting & data
Individual student completion tracking only. No class-wide synthesis, no trend data, no flagging for students who may need support. Teachers see completion, not understanding.
📚 Tied to curriculum?
Partial  Checks connect to their lesson content only — teachers using CharacterStrong cannot use Second Step's assessment features
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
Limited  Some grade levels include Tier 2 extension activities, but no automated tier identification or flagging
✨ Unique features
Longest-standing, most widely adopted SEL curriculum in the US · Strong research base and CASEL alignment documentation · Family take-home materials tied to each lesson
Key Gap
The closest model — but their assessment is low-fidelity and locked to their own content. No tier identification, no class-level synthesis, no "what next." This is the most instructive comparison: CharacterStrong has the same starting point but the opportunity to go much further.
ClassDojo
classdojo.com · Behavior management & parent communication
Free classroom behavior management and parent communication platform used by over 50 million teachers, students, and families worldwide. Teachers award or remove "points" for behaviors and share class moments with parents in real time. Often conflated with SEL tools because of its behavior focus — but behavior tracking is not skill assessment.
📢 Behavior tool
📐 Grade range
K–8; most popular in elementary (K–5)
👤 Primary user
Teachers and parents — designed around parent-facing communication as much as classroom management
📝 How assessment works
Teacher-observed behavior logging — tapping a button to award/deduct points for pre-defined behaviors. This is observation, not assessment. Measures compliance signals, not skill acquisition.
📊 Reporting & data
Class and individual behavior reports; parent-facing portfolio. Data is anecdotal and teacher-defined — no standardization, no benchmarks across classrooms.
📚 Tied to curriculum?
No  Has added some SEL mini-lessons (Big Life Journal content) but these are entirely separate from any SEL curriculum a teacher uses
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
No  No benchmarks, no tier flagging, no intervention guidance — behavior patterns could theoretically inform decisions but the platform provides no structure for this
✨ Unique features
Real-time parent communication (photos, messages, updates) · Free — massive adoption due to zero cost barrier · Class Story feature for sharing classroom culture moments
Key Gap
Behavior points ≠ SEL skill acquisition. Giving a student a "Respect" point doesn't measure whether they've learned what respect means or can apply it in a new situation. Not designed for instruction or learning — designed for management and communication.
Formative / Nearpod
goformative.com · nearpod.com · General formative assessment
General-purpose formative assessment platforms that let teachers embed live quizzes, polls, and open-ended questions into any lesson. Both are fully content-agnostic — teachers create all their own questions. Technically capable but require the most work from teachers to apply to SEL content, and give them the least back.
📋 Assessment tool
📐 Grade range
K–12
👤 Primary user
Classroom teachers building and running formative checks across any subject area
📝 How assessment works
Student-completed: multiple choice, short answer, drawing, polls, matching. Teachers build all questions manually. Results appear live on teacher view as students respond.
📊 Reporting & data
Real-time class view showing correct/incorrect responses; individual breakdown after session. No longitudinal tracking, no curriculum sequence integration, no automated interpretation.
📚 Tied to curriculum?
No — intentionally  Content-agnostic by design. A teacher could use it with CharacterStrong but must write all questions from scratch with zero guidance on what to ask or how to interpret SEL responses
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
No  No benchmarks, no tier flagging, no connection to support frameworks. Teacher must interpret and act entirely on their own.
✨ Unique features
Real-time live class results during instruction · Wide question variety (draw, collaborate, annotate) · Nearpod: immersive content including VR field trips and 3D models
Key Gap
The most technically capable assessment tool here — but entirely teacher-built and content-agnostic. No SEL skill framework, no scope & sequence awareness, no tier support, no interpretation logic. Maximum teacher effort; minimum instructional guidance returned.
The Opportunity
CharacterStrong · Native Assessment Platform · Built on Intellispark Pro
Every tool above solves a different problem. None of them solve the teacher's problem.
Primary user
Classroom teachers — the only user no one in this space is actually building for
How assessment works
Quick checks embedded directly in CharacterStrong lessons — no separate login, no teacher setup, no context switch
Reporting & data
Post-lesson class results designed to answer one question: "Is this class ready to move forward, or do specific students need more support?"
Tied to curriculum?
Yes — natively. Questions align to each lesson's skill. Results connect directly to the scope & sequence, surfacing a recommended next lesson or RESPOND activity.
MTSS / Tier support?
Built in — not as a separate screening cycle, but as an output of normal lesson delivery. Consistent gaps flag students for Tier 2 support automatically.
Grade range
Mirrors CharacterStrong's scope & sequence — Pre-K through 5th grade across Be Kind, Be Strong, and Be Well domains
What's different
Second Step is the closest model — but locked to their curriculum and low-fidelity. Panorama and DESSA solve admin and specialist problems. ClassDojo tracks behavior, not skills. Formative tools require teachers to do all the work. CharacterStrong's opportunity: close the teach → assess → adjust loop natively, for the teacher who's already in the platform.
The question to validate first: Is the problem that teachers lack access to assessment data — or that they have data and don't know what to do with it? Discovery interviews will answer this before any build decisions are made.
Key Findings
0 / 5
Embedded in SEL Curriculum
No competitor offers assessment native to an SEL curriculum. Every tool requires a context switch — friction teachers won't sustain.
3 / 5
Solving Admin Problems, Not Teacher Problems
Panorama, DESSA, and ClassDojo are primarily designed to give administrators and specialists data — not to help a classroom teacher decide what to do next week.
1 / 5
MTSS Tier Support
Only DESSA identifies Tier 2/3 needs — through a quarterly teacher-rating process. No tool surfaces tier needs in the moment of learning, tied to actual curriculum content.
