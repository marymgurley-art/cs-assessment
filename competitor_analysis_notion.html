Scope note: Intellispark Pro (CharacterStrong's active integration, launched Winter 2025) is excluded. This analysis covers the broader SEL assessment market — understanding what each tool actually solves, who it's really for, and where the gaps are.
01 Competitors
Panorama provides districts with SEL survey tools and data dashboards that help administrators measure student social-emotional well-being at scale. Used by thousands of districts to report SEL outcomes to school boards and funders.
📐 Grade range?
K–12
👤 Primary user?
Administrators and district leaders — teachers are the data collectors but not the primary audience for results
📝 How does assessment work?
Student-completed surveys (typically 10–20 questions) administered 2–3× per year. Measures SEL competencies like self-management, social awareness, and growth mindset.
📊 How does reporting/data work?
District-wide dashboards showing SEL trends by school, grade, and demographic group. Designed for leadership presentations and compliance reporting — not daily instructional use.
📚 Tied to curriculum or instruction?
No. Surveys are standalone — completely disconnected from what teachers are actually teaching. A teacher running a Panorama survey doesn't connect it to any specific lesson or unit.
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
Limited. Aggregate data can inform tier decisions at the school level but doesn't identify individual students needing Tier 2/3 support in any structured way.
✨ Unique features?
  • Demographic disaggregation (race, gender, ELL status)
  • Family and staff surveys alongside student surveys
  • Longitudinal trend reporting across school years
Key gap: Solves the district reporting problem, not the teacher instruction problem. 2–3× per year misses every instructional moment. Teachers get no guidance on what to do after seeing results. Fundamentally an admin tool disguised as an SEL tool.
Aperture's DESSA (Devereux Student Strengths Assessment) is a standardized, norm-referenced SEL screening tool used in MTSS frameworks. It gives schools research-backed data for identifying students who need Tier 2 or Tier 3 SEL intervention — primarily used by school counselors, psychologists, and MTSS teams.
📐 Grade range?
K–8 (DESSA); additional versions for Pre-K and high school
👤 Primary user?
School counselors, psychologists, MTSS specialists — classroom teachers complete ratings but are not the primary audience. Results flow to intervention teams, not back to teachers.
📝 How does assessment work?
Teachers complete a 72-item behavioral rating scale for each student, typically 3× per year. Ratings are scored against national norms to identify students above or below benchmark.
📊 How does reporting/data work?
Individual student profiles showing SEL strengths and needs across 8 competency areas. Designed for IEP documentation, intervention planning, and compliance reporting — not real-time instructional decisions.
📚 Tied to curriculum or instruction?
No. Purely a screening tool. It identifies that a student has a gap but provides no guidance on how to address it through instruction — or which curriculum to use.
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
Yes — this is its core purpose. Strong normative benchmarks for Tier identification. Best-in-class for formal MTSS documentation. Limited in guiding what teachers should actually teach next.
✨ Unique features?
  • Norm-referenced national benchmarks — defensible for IEP and legal documentation
  • Strengths-based framing (measures assets, not just deficits)
  • Research-validated across 15+ years of use
Key gap: Designed for identification, not instruction. Rating 20–30 students every semester is a heavy burden — and after completing it, teachers get no actionable guidance for their classroom. Tells you who needs help; doesn't help you help them.
Second Step is a K–8 evidence-based SEL curriculum from Committee for Children — the closest direct competitor to CharacterStrong. It provides full lesson plans, student materials, and teacher guides organized around four core SEL competency areas. Some digital versions include basic embedded comprehension checks.
📐 Grade range?
Pre-K – Grade 8; high school version (Step Up to High School) sold separately
👤 Primary user?
Classroom teachers — this is the most direct analog to CharacterStrong's user
📝 How does assessment work?
Low-fidelity embedded checks within digital lesson modules — mostly multiple choice comprehension questions. Not a systematic assessment framework; varies by grade and unit. No class-wide data view.
📊 How does reporting/data work?
Minimal. Individual student completion tracking in digital version. No class-wide synthesis, no trend data, no flag for students who may need support. Teachers see completion, not understanding.
📚 Tied to curriculum or instruction?
Yes — but only within their own curriculum. Checks connect to their lesson content. Teachers using CharacterStrong cannot use Second Step's assessment features at all.
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
Limited. Some grade levels include Tier 2 extension activities, but no automated tier identification or flagging. Teachers must identify struggling students manually.
✨ Unique features?
  • Longest-standing, most widely adopted SEL curriculum in the US
  • Strong research base and CASEL alignment documentation
  • Family take-home materials tied to each lesson
Key gap: The closest model — but their assessment is low-fidelity and locked to their own content. No tier identification, no class-level synthesis, no "what next." A teacher using CharacterStrong gets nothing from Second Step's approach. This is the most instructive comparison for what CharacterStrong should build — and do better.
ClassDojo is a free classroom behavior management and parent communication platform used by over 50 million teachers, students, and families worldwide. Teachers award or remove "points" for behaviors and share class moments with parents in real time. Sometimes conflated with SEL tools because of its behavior focus.
📐 Grade range?
K–8, though most popular in elementary (K–5)
👤 Primary user?
Teachers and parents — the product is designed around parent-facing communication as much as classroom management. Students interact primarily to see their own point tallies.
📝 How does assessment work?
Teacher-observed behavior logging — a teacher taps a button to award or deduct points for pre-defined behaviors (e.g., "Teamwork," "Helping Others"). This is observation, not assessment. It measures compliance signals, not skill acquisition.
📊 How does reporting/data work?
Class and individual student behavior reports; parent-facing portfolio showing class moments and student progress. Data is anecdotal and teacher-defined — no standardization across classrooms.
📚 Tied to curriculum or instruction?
No. ClassDojo has added some SEL mini-lessons ("Big Life Journal" content) but these are entirely separate from any SEL curriculum a teacher is using. There is no connection to CharacterStrong lessons.
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
No formal support. Behavior patterns could theoretically inform tier decisions but the platform provides no structure for this — no benchmarks, no flagging, no intervention guidance.
✨ Unique features?
  • Real-time parent communication (photos, messages, updates)
  • Free — massive adoption because of zero cost barrier
  • Class Story feature for sharing classroom culture moments
Key gap: Behavior points ≠ SEL skill acquisition. Giving a student a "Respect" point doesn't mean they've learned what respect means or can apply it in a new situation. Not designed for instruction or learning — designed for management and communication.
Formative and Nearpod are general-purpose formative assessment platforms that let teachers embed live quizzes, polls, and open-ended questions into any lesson. Both are content-agnostic — teachers create or import their own questions. Widely used across all subject areas in K–12.
📐 Grade range?
K–12
👤 Primary user?
Teachers — building and running formative checks. Students complete the activities in real time from their devices.
📝 How does assessment work?
Student-completed: multiple choice, short answer, drawing, polls, matching. Teachers build questions manually or import from question banks. Results appear live on teacher view as students respond.
📊 How does reporting/data work?
Real-time class view showing how many students answered correctly; individual student breakdown after session. No longitudinal tracking, no integration with any curriculum sequence, no automated interpretation.
📚 Tied to curriculum or instruction?
No — intentionally content-agnostic. A teacher could use it with CharacterStrong lessons but would need to write all questions from scratch with zero guidance on what to ask or how to interpret answers in an SEL context.
🏷️ MTSS / Tier support?
No. No benchmarks, no tier flagging, no connection to support frameworks. Raw data is available but the teacher must interpret and act on it entirely on their own.
✨ Unique features?
  • Real-time live class results during instruction
  • Wide variety of question types (draw, collaborate, etc.)
  • Nearpod: immersive content like VR field trips, 3D models
Key gap: The most technically capable assessment tool on the list — but entirely teacher-built and content-agnostic. No SEL skill framework, no scope & sequence, no tier support, no interpretation logic. Requires the most work from teachers to use with SEL content and gives them the least back.
02 The Opportunity
🎯
Every tool above either solves the admin reporting problem, the MTSS screening problem, the behavior management problem, or the general assessment problem. None of them solve the teacher instruction problem — what should I do next Monday based on what I just learned about my students?
✅
CharacterStrong Native Assessment — Built on Intellispark Pro
Primary user?
Classroom teachers — the only user in this space no one is actually building for
How does assessment work?
Quick checks embedded directly in CharacterStrong lessons — students complete them without leaving the platform, no separate login, no extra teacher setup
How does reporting work?
Post-lesson class results with individual student breakdown — designed to answer one question: "Is this class ready to move forward, or do specific students need more support?"
Tied to curriculum or instruction?
Yes — natively. Questions are aligned to each lesson's skill. Results connect directly to the scope & sequence — so the platform can surface a recommended next lesson or activity rather than leaving the teacher to decide alone
MTSS / Tier support?
Built in — not as a separate screening process but as an output of normal lesson delivery. Students who consistently show gaps get flagged for Tier 2 support without teachers having to run a separate assessment cycle
What's different?
First platform to close the teach → assess → adjust loop inside an SEL curriculum. The data informs instruction; instruction informs data. Neither exists in isolation.
